## Design Context

### Users
**Primary**: Content creators — YouTubers, streamers, musicians, and influencers who need to be convinced to bring their fanbase onto Castar. They are sophisticated, brand-aware, and evaluate products on legitimacy and revenue potential. They've seen plenty of creator-economy pitches and are skeptical of anything that feels gimmicky or fly-by-night.

**Secondary**: Fans — Gen Z and Millennial consumers who already spend money on their favorite creators. They respond to energy, community, and status (XP, leaderboards). They need to feel the product is for *them*, not just another subscription.

**Job to be done**: Creators → "Show me a credible business model that makes my fans money without asking them to open their wallets." Fans → "Let me support my favorite creator without it costing me anything extra."

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Bold, Credible, Community

**Tone**: High-energy but financially trustworthy — the intersection of a product Gen Z would actually use and one a creator could confidently recommend to their audience without it feeling like a scam. Think Robinhood's optimism and Stripe's craft, applied to a gamified fan-economy product.

**Emotional goals**: Creators should feel excited and *safe* — this is real money, real infrastructure. Fans should feel like they're part of something exclusive and rewarding.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual tone**: Bold and energetic with refined polish. Clean, confident layouts with strong typographic hierarchy. Dark backgrounds are acceptable but must feel premium, not crypto. High contrast, purposeful color usage.

**References**: Robinhood (optimistic, modern, trustworthy), Stripe (exceptional craft, clear hierarchy, confident simplicity)

**Anti-references**:
- Crypto / Web3: NO neon-on-black, no glowing purple accents, no "to the moon" energy
- Kid's app: NO overly cute or cartoonish elements — this is a real financial product
- Corporate enterprise: NO stiff, blue-gradient, stock-photo banking aesthetic
- Generic fintech SaaS: NO trust badges galore, generic sans-serifs, safe palette with no point of view

### Design Principles
1. **Creator-first credibility**: Every section should answer "why should a creator trust this?" before "why should a fan use this?" — legitimacy and revenue numbers front and center.
2. **Energy without noise**: Bold doesn't mean chaotic. Use strong typographic moments, confident color, and intentional white space. Resist adding more visual elements when restraint creates impact.
3. **Financial confidence**: The product handles real money. Design must signal reliability — precision in layout, clean data presentation, no gimmicks that would make a creator hesitate.
4. **Distinctive, not derivative**: Avoid every AI-generated fintech trope — gradient text everywhere, glassmorphism cards, identical metric grids, neon accent on dark. If it looks like it could have been made for any other fintech, it's wrong.
5. **Community as visual language**: The product is about belonging and identity. Where possible, let the design evoke fandom and community — not through cartoons, but through bold personality and expressive typography.
